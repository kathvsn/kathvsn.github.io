Selling White As Right

The Cosmetic Industry's Promotion of Colorism


i s s u e

For decades, the cosmetic industry has made a profit off of people of color while simultaneously excluding them. Skin lightening products are one of the best selling products in the industry and it is only right to call out the ethical morality of cosmetic companies who promote colorism through the mass production and promotion of these harmful products.


p r o m o t i o n

When advertising and promoting these skin lightening products, there is always the notion that being lighter equates to being beautiful. Certain words associated with whiteness are used to attract consumers to evoke interest and persuade them into purchasing a product. This fabricated idea of beauty is incredibly harmful, especially to young and impressionable people of color.


h e l p

There are various ways that consumers can help to bring more awareness to the issue at hand. We have the power to stop supporting cosmetic companies who partake in this practice. Instead of creating and promoting products that portray this illusion of inclusion, we can call on cosmetic companies to focus their efforts on actually developing more products for a diverse range of skin tones.
