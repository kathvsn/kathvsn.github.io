Why Should We Help?

An issue that people with dark skin tones face is the lack of cosmetics that match their skin tone. People argue that the issue of diversity in the cosmetics industry is not due to the lack of products suitable for all skin tones themselves, rather the issue is the lack of effort companies put into creating these products. As consumers, we have the power to boycott these companies as we are the ones providing them with a market to make a profit from.

We should help because as colorism persists in racial and ethnic communities, people will continue to bleach their skin to achieve a lighter complexion and the demand for skin lightening products will increase. Cosmetic companies will meet the demands for these products and will advertise them to be an effective way to become lighter and beautiful, thus promoting colorism. The discrimination towards people with darker skin continues as this cycle repeats itself.

Resolving this issue is in no way easy, but the cosmetic companies who feed into the issue of colorism by selling these controversial products need to be held accountable for their part in promoting colorism. Without coordinated global effort against colorist advertising, including social media and other consumer activism, boycotts alone are likely to be ineffective. The least we can do is support ethical companies that are run by people of color looking to embrace racial inclusivity.
